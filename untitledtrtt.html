MCQs - Muscular System

1. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Skeletal muscle is derived from splanchnic mesoderm.
  • b) Smooth muscle differentiates only from mesoderm.
  • c) Cardiac muscle is derived from paraxial mesoderm.
  • d) Skeletal muscle is derived from paraxial mesoderm.
  • e) Smooth muscle differentiates solely from ectoderm.
Correct Answer: d) Skeletal muscle is derived from paraxial mesoderm.

2. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Head musculature is derived from somites.
  • b) Musculature of the limbs is derived from somitomeres.
  • c) Head musculature is derived from somitomeres.
  • d) Musculature of the axial skeleton is derived from lateral plate mesoderm.
  • e) Head musculature is derived from sclerotome.
Correct Answer: c) Head musculature is derived from somitomeres.

3. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Infrahyoid muscles are derived from the dorsomedial (DML) lip.
  • b) Limb muscles are derived from the dorsomedial (DML) lip.
  • c) Back muscles are derived from the ventrolateral (VLL) region.
  • d) Abdominal wall muscles are derived from the ventrolateral (VLL) region.
  • e) Intercostal muscles are derived from the ventrolateral (VLL) region.
Correct Answer: d) Abdominal wall muscles are derived from the ventrolateral (VLL) region.

4. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) The primaxial domain includes somite-derived cells that migrated across the lateral somitic frontier.
  • b) The abaxial domain contains cells that receive developmental signals from the neural tube and notochord.
  • c) The lateral somitic frontier defines the border between dermis derived from somites and lateral plate mesoderm.
  • d) The primaxial domain consists of lateral plate mesoderm.
  • e) The abaxial domain contains only somite-derived cells.
Correct Answer: c) The lateral somitic frontier defines the border between dermis derived from somites and lateral plate mesoderm.

5. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) The bony components of ribs are derived from lateral plate mesoderm.
  • b) The cartilaginous part of ribs attached to the sternum is derived from primaxial cells.
  • c) The bony components of ribs are derived from primaxial sclerotome cells.
  • d) The cartilaginous part of ribs not attached to the sternum is derived from abaxial cells.
  • e) Rib development is not influenced by the lateral somitic frontier.
Correct Answer: c) The bony components of ribs are derived from primaxial sclerotome cells.

6. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Epimeres are innervated by ventral primary rami.
  • b) Hypomeres are innervated by dorsal primary rami.
  • c) The old concept of muscle development focuses on abaxial and primaxial cells.
  • d) The new concept of muscle development is based on the functional definition of innervation.
  • e) Hypomeres are innervated by ventral primary rami.
Correct Answer: e) Hypomeres are innervated by ventral primary rami.

7. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Myoblasts do not fuse during skeletal muscle differentiation.
  • b) Tendons are derived from dermatomes.
  • c) Myofibrils appear before the fusion of myoblasts.
  • d) Tendons are derived from sclerotome cells lying adjacent to myotomes.
  • e) Cross-striations in muscle fibers appear by the end of the first month.
Correct Answer: d) Tendons are derived from sclerotome cells lying adjacent to myotomes.

8. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Connective tissues in the head region are derived from somitic mesoderm.
  • b) Connective tissues in the cervical region are derived from neural crest cells.
  • c) Connective tissues in the body wall are derived from neural crest cells.
  • d) Connective tissues in the limbs are derived from the parietal layer of lateral plate mesoderm.
  • e) Myoblasts in the head region are derived from neural crest cells.
Correct Answer: d) Connective tissues in the limbs are derived from the parietal layer of lateral plate mesoderm.

9. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) All voluntary muscles of the head are derived from ectoderm.
  • b) The iris muscle is derived from paraxial mesoderm.
  • c) Patterns of muscle formation in the head are directed by neural crest cells.
  • d) The tongue muscles are derived from ectoderm.
  • e) The pharyngeal arch muscles are derived from sclerotome.
Correct Answer: c) Patterns of muscle formation in the head are directed by neural crest cells.

10. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Limb musculature is derived from neural crest cells.
  • b) The mesenchyme forming limb muscles is derived from lateral plate mesoderm.
  • c) The connective tissue in the limbs is derived from neural crest cells.
  • d) Limb bones are derived from somites.
  • e) The first indication of limb musculature appears in the 7th week of development.
Correct Answer: e) The first indication of limb musculature appears in the 7th week of development.

11. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Cardiac muscle develops from paraxial mesoderm.
  • b) Myoblasts in cardiac muscle fuse during development.
  • c) Purkinje fibers are formed from splanchnic mesoderm.
  • d) Cardiac muscle develops from splanchnic mesoderm surrounding the heart tube.
  • e) Intercalated discs in cardiac muscle are formed by fusion of myoblasts.
Correct Answer: d) Cardiac muscle develops from splanchnic mesoderm surrounding the heart tube.

12. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Smooth muscle of the dorsal aorta is derived from paraxial mesoderm.
  • b) Smooth muscle in the wall of the gut tube is derived from ectoderm.
  • c) Smooth muscle in the coronary arteries originates from proepicardial cells and neural crest cells.
  • d) The sphincter and dilator muscles of the pupil are derived from mesoderm.
  • e) Smooth muscle in the large arteries is derived from ectoderm.
Correct Answer: c) Smooth muscle in the coronary arteries originates from proepicardial cells and neural crest cells.

13. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Complete absence of muscle is always debilitating.
  • b) Poland sequence is characterized by bilateral aplasia of the sternocostal head of the pectoralis major muscle.
  • c) Prune belly syndrome involves partial or complete absence of chest musculature.
  • d) Duchenne's muscular dystrophy is an X-linked recessive disease.
  • e) Poland sequence occurs more frequently among females than males.
Correct Answer: d) Duchenne's muscular dystrophy is an X-linked recessive disease.

14. All of the following are false except one true:

  • a) Myoblasts fuse to form multinucleated muscle fibers in cardiac muscle.
  • b) Myofibrils do not appear during the differentiation of skeletal muscle.
  • c) Cross-striations typical of skeletal muscle appear by the end of the third month.
  • d) Tendons for muscle attachment to bones are derived from dermatomes.
  • e) Somitomeres segregate into sclerotome and dermomyotome segments in the head region.
Correct Answer: c) Cross-striations typical of skeletal muscle appear by the end of the third month.

15. All of the following are false except one true:
